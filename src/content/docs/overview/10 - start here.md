## History

- Founded in 1972 by Elbert Eugene "Gene" Spriggs Jr. (born in 1937, died in 2021, and known within the group as "Yônêq").
- His 3rd wife Marsha (known as "Hâ-Êmeq" in the group) was very influntial and is still alive today.
- Gene had [one son (Tyrone) from his first wife](https://psmag.com/social-justice/inside-the-twelve-tribes/#:~:text=A%20large%20portion%20of%20Spriggs'%20teachings%20concerned,son%20and%20hates%20the%20word%20of%20God.%E2%80%9D), but had no children with Marsha (and so no children in the group).

- Gene's original right-hand man was Eddie Wiseman. Eddie's wife died, and all of his children have left the Tribes. This means that both the founder and cofounder have no children in the group.

- The group was originally called the "Vine Christian Community Church" (Chattanooga, Tennessee).

- They received pushback locally, so they moved to Island Pond (Vermont) and renamed themselves "Northeast Kingdom Community Church." The reason of the resistance in Chattanooga, depending on whether the Tribes are telling the story or the local residents of Chattanooga are telling it.

- The group renamed themselves again, this time as the "Twelve Tribes Communities."

- They then established communities in various parts of the world (different regions of the US and the world were identified as "tribes").


## Activities

- Recruitment and public outreach often happen in local communities (especially on Friday evenings), their Yellow Deli restaurant chain, "Open Forum" events, and in large traveling buses and boat.

- When someone joins, they move in with the Tribes, give the Tribes all their belongings and money, and receive a new Hebrew name.

- The Tribes also make money via their restaurants, construction businesses, food sales, furniture sales, etc.

- Membership declined when it came out that the founder's wife had sexual relationships with multiple younger men in the group on multiple continents, over a 20-year period. Further dissolutionment came from additional situations, include an elder being convicted of child rape and battery, an on-site homocide, and the death of the founder.
