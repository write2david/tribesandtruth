## Culture
- Chopsticks

- Accountability = covering

- Ponytails for men

- Dress code

- Twice daily gatherings: The Minchah (Daily Gatherings): Visitors staying with the community are not only permitted but required to attend the morning and evening minchah.

- Outsiders are typically not allowed to be present for the "Breaking of Bread" nor for "councils," "apostolic workers meetings," and "intertribal meetings" where leaders gather to discuss doctrine, legal issues, or community management.

- Friday Night (Erev Shabbat) Celebrations: These are often used as "evangelistic events" specifically designed to host visitors, neighbors, and potential converts.

- Shunning 

- Breaking of Bread: Saturday night at 10pm, only for baptized members. This is a candle lit ceremony, expanded beyond the traditional Lord's Supper. Feet are washed, shoes and socks taken off. Soup with the bread, but not allowed to dip bread in soup. Bowing, victories and defeats from the week, prayer, singing.
