## Culture

- COMMUNE: When joining the group, new members give the group all their belongings and move in. Members live together in large, extended-family households. No one has private posessions, income, or bank accounts.

- HEBREW: New members are given new Hebrew names. Hebrew equivalents are also  used for words like: mom, dad, Jesus, God, church, etc.

- GATHERINGS: Twice a day, the community gathers for worship, singing, and dancing.

- ACCOUNTABILITY: Everyone must have a spiritual authority or "covering" that provides guidance and accountability.

- DRESS CODE:  Members wear simple clothing without designs or messages on them. Women wear dresses (or "sus pants") and head coverings. Men must have beards, with their hair  tied back in a ponytail roughly the size of a fist.

- FOOD:  Primarily homemade meals, eaten with chopsticks in order to appeal to Asians and for cleanliness. Friday evening (beginning of the Sabbath) is the biggest meal of the week. 

- FRIDAY NIGHT: The Sabbath begins at sundown with a festive celebration designed for evangelism.  They feature shared meals, circle dancing, and folk music.

- SATURDAY NIGHT: The "Breaking of Bread" begins at 10pm (only for baptized members). This is a candle lit ceremony, expanded beyond the traditional Lord's Supper. Feet are washed, shoes and socks taken off. Soup with the bread, but not allowed to dip bread in soup. Bowing, victories and defeats from the week, prayer, singing.

- PUBLIC RESTRICTED:  While very hospitable in public, outsiders are typically not allowed to be present for the "Breaking of Bread" nor for "councils," "apostolic workers meetings," and "intertribal meetings" where leaders gather to discuss doctrine, legal issues, or community management.


- SHUNNING: Independent thinking or dissent is viewed as a spiritual threat. Rebellious members who don't accept correction are sent away from the community, with their eternal destiny at risk.
